About / Victoria Hong

Designing where people, systems, and technology meet.

I’m a creative technologist bridging design and engineering to create intuitive, human-centered experiences. My work sits at the intersection of product strategy, UX research, front-end development, and emerging AI systems.

How I work

I turn complex workflows into clear, useful product experiences.

My background spans product design, software development, UX research, and systems engineering. I enjoy working through ambiguity: understanding user pain points, mapping workflows, prototyping ideas, and building interfaces that make difficult tasks feel easier to complete.

Because I can move between research, design, and implementation, I think deeply about both the user experience and the system behind it. I care about products that are not only visually thoughtful, but also scalable, accessible, and grounded in real user needs.

01 / Research

Start with people.

I use interviews, observation, surveys, and usability testing to understand what users actually need before deciding what to build.

02 / Design

Simplify the system.

I translate messy workflows into user flows, information architecture, wireframes, prototypes, and design systems.

03 / Build

Make it real.

I bring designs into production with front-end development, component thinking, and close collaboration across engineering and product teams.

My path

A background shaped by engineering, design, and community.

I began my journey in engineering, where I learned how to break down complex problems and design systems with intention. Over time, I became increasingly drawn to the human side of technology: how people use products, where workflows break down, and how thoughtful design can make digital experiences feel more intuitive.

Today, that combination shapes how I work. I approach design as both a creative and technical practice: one that requires empathy, structure, experimentation, and a deep respect for the people using the product.
