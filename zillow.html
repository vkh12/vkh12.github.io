Overview

Redesigning apartment search for shared decision-making.

Most apartment search platforms are designed around one user’s preferences. However, many people search for housing with roommates, where budget, commute, safety, amenities, and neighborhood preferences must be negotiated across multiple people.

The challenge

Apartment platforms help individuals search, but roommate groups still make decisions outside the product.

Roommate groups often rely on screenshots, text messages, spreadsheets, and separate searches to compare apartments. This makes it difficult to understand tradeoffs, align priorities, and decide which listing works best for everyone.

How might we

help roommate groups search, compare, and decide on apartments together within one platform?

The solution

A collaborative apartment search experience.

The redesigned Zillow experience introduces shared preferences, group-based ranking, and collaborative review tools so roommates can move from scattered coordination to a clearer shared decision-making process.

Process

From individual search to multi-stakeholder relevance.

I approached the redesign through an HCI and information retrieval lens, focusing on how relevance changes when multiple users must agree on one shared outcome.

01 Research

Studied apartment search behavior and collaborative decision-making pain points.

02 Frame

Defined the problem around preference conflict, fairness, and group relevance.

03 Design

Created user flows, ranking logic, wireframes, and collaborative search features.

04 Refine

Improved clarity around compatibility scores, shared preferences, and group actions.

Key insight

Designing for groups means designing for negotiation, not just discovery.

A strong apartment search experience should not only return relevant listings. It should help people understand why a listing works, who it works for, and what tradeoffs still need to be discussed.

Reflection

This project helped me understand that relevance is not always individual. In collaborative decisions, the best result is the one that supports alignment, compromise, and trust.
